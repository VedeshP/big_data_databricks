Now, this transcript introduces a massive paradigm shift in the data world. We are moving from **ETL** to **ELT (Extract, Load, Transform)**. 

If you want to master Databricks, you *must* understand ELT, because Databricks and the modern cloud basically forced the entire industry to switch to this model.

Here is the conceptual breakdown and why it matters.

---

### 1. The Core Theory: ETL vs. ELT

To understand the difference, you have to look at **where the processing (computing) power lives.**

**The Old Way: ETL (Extract -> Transform -> Load)**
*   *How it worked:* You have a Source Database (Oracle), a Transformation Server (like Informatica, Slide 12), and a Target Data Warehouse (Teradata). Data leaves the source, travels over the network to the Informatica server, gets transformed in Informatica's memory, and then travels over the network *again* to be loaded into the Data Warehouse.
*   *The Problem (Slide 10):* It requires an expensive, dedicated server just to do the math (Transformation). If you have petabytes of Big Data, the network chokes trying to move it all around. 

**The Modern Way: ELT (Extract -> Load -> Transform)**
*   *How it works (Slide 6):* You extract the raw data and **Load it immediately** into your target storage. Then, you use the massive computing power of the target system itself to do the Transformations.
*   *The Benefits (Slide 9):* You don't need a middle-man server. You utilize the hardware you already have. Furthermore, because you loaded *all* the raw data first, if the business later decides they want a new column, you don't have to go back to the source system to re-extract it. It’s already sitting in your storage!

### 2. The Outdated Perspectives in the Transcript
Keep in mind this presentation is from 2020 and reflects slightly older enterprise views. 
*   Slide 11 says ELT has "limited tools available" and is an "evolving approach." In 2024/2025, **ELT is the absolute industry standard**. Almost no modern tech company does traditional ETL anymore. 

---

### 🚀 The "Databricks Philosophy" Connection

Databricks was custom-built for the **ELT** paradigm. In fact, the famous **Medallion Architecture** (Bronze -> Silver -> Gold) we keep talking about is just a fancy implementation of ELT!

Here is how Databricks perfectly executes ELT:

**1. The "L" happens first (The Bronze Layer):**
Because cloud storage (AWS S3, Azure ADLS) is unbelievably cheap, Databricks best practices tell you to **Extract** data from your source APIs/databases and **Load** it directly into your Bronze layer as raw JSON or Parquet files. Don't touch it, don't clean it, just get it into the Data Lake as fast as possible. 

**2. The "T" happens in place (Spark & Databricks SQL):**
Now that the data is resting safely in your Data Lake, you use Databricks' massive computing engine (Apache Spark) to **Transform** the data into Silver and Gold tables. The data never has to leave the Databricks ecosystem over a slow network; the Spark workers just read the files, do the math, and save them back as new tables.

**3. The Rise of `dbt` (Data Build Tool):**
When you start doing practical Databricks work, you will likely hear about a tool called **dbt**. dbt is the most popular ELT tool in the world right now. It allows data analysts to write simple SQL SELECT statements, and it pushes the heavy computing down into the Databricks SQL engine to do the transformations. Databricks and dbt are a match made in ELT heaven.
